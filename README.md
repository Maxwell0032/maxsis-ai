\# Maxsis AI



\### AI Financial Intelligence for Institutional Transparency



Maxsis AI is an open-source financial intelligence engine that helps

nonprofits, small businesses, and public-interest organizations detect

financial stress early by automatically analyzing operational financial data.



The system translates raw financial records into risk signals and plain-language

narrative insights that support proactive decision-making and accountability.



---



\## Why This Matters



Organizations serving vulnerable populations often lack access to continuous

financial oversight. Problems such as grant reporting gaps, liquidity crises,

and undetected budget anomalies can lead to:



\- disruption of essential services

\- loss of public funding

\- delayed corrective action

\- increased financial stress on frontline workers



Maxsis AI is designed to lower the barrier to institutional-grade financial

intelligence so that smaller organizations can intervene before financial

instability becomes a crisis.



---



\## Current Prototype



This repository contains a working Python proof-of-concept that:



\- ingests structured financial CSV data

\- calculates burn rate and estimated liquidity runway

\- detects abnormal financial pressure using anomaly scoring

\- estimates simple revenue concentration risk signals

\- generates a CFO-style narrative risk summary



The prototype demonstrates how automated financial intelligence can improve

organizational transparency and resilience.



---



\## Example Output



The system produces narrative insights such as:



\- estimated liquidity runway

\- anomaly signals in financial performance

\- risk classification (low / medium / high)

\- recommended stabilization actions



This output is designed to be understandable by non-technical leaders,

including nonprofit executives and program managers.



---



\## Planned Expansion



Future modules under active design include:



\- additional compliance signal libraries for grant-funded programs

\- ingestion support for accounting exports (e.g., QuickBooks)

\- on-chain treasury monitoring tools for digital asset transparency

\- municipal public financial report analysis

\- lightweight web dashboard for accessibility by non-technical users



---



\## Open Source Commitment



During the initial grant period, core financial risk-scoring logic and selected

transparency tools will be released as open source to support civic technology

and institutional accountability initiatives.
